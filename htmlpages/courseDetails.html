Course Details




Intermediate


Our Music schools offer intermediate courses for students who have already completed the beginner level and have a basic understanding of music theory and proficiency in playing an instrument. Here are some details about intermediate level music courses.


Music Theory:



Intermediate level music theory classes focus on building a stronger understanding of harmony, chord progressions, and key signatures. Students will learn about cadences, secondary dominants, modulation, and other advanced concepts.


course Duration: 6 months

course Fee: $100


Instrumental Instruction:



Intermediate instrumental instruction classes focus on improving technique, developing sight-reading skills, and building a repertoire of pieces in different styles. Students will learn to play more complex pieces and will work on developing their interpretation and expression.


course Duration: 6 months

course Fee: $120


Ear Training:



Intermediate ear training classes focus on developing a more sophisticated sense of pitch, interval, and chord recognition. Students will learn to recognize and transcribe more complex melodies and harmonies.


course Duration: 6 months

course Fee: $110


Ensemble Performance:



Intermediate ensemble performance classes give students the opportunity to play with other musicians in small groups or ensembles. Students will learn to listen and respond to other musicians, develop ensemble skills, and perform more challenging repertoire.


course Duration: 6 months

course Fee: $90

Degree


Our Music schools offer degree courses for students who want to pursue a career in music or continue their education in music at a higher level. The degree programs offered by music schools typically include undergraduate and graduate programs. Here are some details about degree level music courses:


Music Theory and Composition:



Degree level courses in music theory and composition focus on developing advanced skills in music theory, harmony, counterpoint, and composition. Students will study advanced techniques in music composition, orchestration, and arranging


course Duration: 2 years

course Fee: $800


Music Technology:



Degree level music technology courses focus on the use of technology in music production, recording, and composition. Students will learn about digital audio workstations, MIDI programming, and sound design. They will also receive instruction in music theory and performance.


course Duration: 1 year

course Fee: $500


Music Education:



Degree level music education courses are designed for students who want to become music teachers. Students will learn about music curriculum development, classroom management, and teaching techniques. They will also receive instruction in music theory, performance, and conducting.


course Duration: 2 years

course Fee: $900


Musicology:



Degree level musicology courses are designed for students who want to pursue research in music history and theory. Students will study the history of music, music theory, and analysis of musical works. They will also learn research methods and techniques for writing musicology papers and dissertations.


course Duration: 1 year

course Fee: $650

post Graduation


Music schools offer postgraduate courses for students who have completed a bachelor's degree in music or a related field and want to further their education or specialize in a particular area of music. Here are some details about postgraduate level music courses:


Master of Music (MM):



The Master of Music is a postgraduate degree that allows students to specialize in a particular area of music such as performance, composition, music education, or musicology. Students will engage in advanced coursework and research and may be required to complete a thesis or recital.


course Duration: 4 years

course Fee: $650


Doctor of Musical Arts (DMA):



The Doctor of Musical Arts is a terminal degree that is designed for students who want to pursue a career as a professional performer, composer, or music educator. The program typically includes advanced coursework, research, and a final project or dissertation.


course Duration: 4 years

course Fee: $650


Master of Arts (MA) in Music:



The Master of Arts in Music is a postgraduate degree that is designed for students who want to focus on music history, theory, or research. The program typically includes advanced coursework in music history, theory, research methods, and a final thesis or research project.


course Duration: 3 years

course Fee: $650


Graduate Certificate in Music:



A graduate certificate in music is a postgraduate credential that can be earned in a specific area of music such as music education, music performance, or music theory. The program typically includes a focused curriculum in the chosen area of study.


course Duration: 3 years

course Fee: $650


Overall, postgraduate level music courses provide students with advanced education and training in their chosen area of music, preparing them for careers as professional musicians, composers, music educators, or music scholars.
